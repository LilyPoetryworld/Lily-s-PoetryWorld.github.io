Hunter

Many' a' times, you hunt my mind,

Chasing it to the crazy lanes
that lead nowhere,
You devour on my flesh,
You eat me in and out
What do I call thee?
A hungry wolf controlled by the moon
Or a villain that manifests in my melancholy?

Many' a' times you prick me
Out of sleep,
With nightmares that seem
Not to fly away with the blue
Feathers of the dream catcher above my
Bed
What do I call thee?
A monster with sharp nails that whispers negativity in my ears
Or a villain that manifests in my fear?


Many' a' times, you tickle me,
To see me laugh like a maniac
While my eyes bleed with tears
What do I call thee?
A sinister spirit put to unrest
Or a villain that manifests in my irrationality?

In the tussle, sometimes I win
Sometimes you!
And each time you win,
You injure two fragments of my soul,
One where I lie
And one where my beloved does
For he takes your wrath too
And hugs me still
With his chest that you have scarred
It stains my shirt red;
He calls it not blood
But the color of his love

What do I call thee?
A rival that challenges my love
Or a villain that manifests in my zest to defeat it?

Lily
