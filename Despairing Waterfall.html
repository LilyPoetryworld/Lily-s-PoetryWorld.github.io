Despairing Waterfall

Dressed in the shadows of vulnerability I wandered by

With a tattered body and a half-burnt heart,

into the depths of a dark forest

Oh, how the rough grasses prickled my ankle, inflicting a familiar pain!
Oh, how the cold breeze slapped my scarred face, impelling me to retreat!
Nevertheless, along the prison of my ribs, I ran recklessly,
searching for him, adamant to quench a life-long thirst.

My breath halted at the sight of a sunlit waterfall.
It was the water of his love that glistened, creating a sinfonietta of dulcet rhythms while cascading down the ledges.
I sat on the huddle of rocks with my naked feet dipped in cold, frothy dampness.
Waves of his affection slowly washed over my skin, leaving behind small droplets,
reminding me of my homeless tears which longed to fall on his chest.

A thirst like no other fueled the aridness in my throat.
My cupped hands reached out to pour some water into their embrace,
But before the wetness on my palm could grace my lips, the water trickled away.
I tried again and again to drink his love from the cranny of my grasp only to realize that he wasn't mine to behold.

He was the gentle stream that unknowingly fluttered quite often into the river of my heart.
And, I, a "regular visitor", fancied watching him crawl away from the depths of my soul and originate in them once again.
I could touch him, feel him, bathe in him but never consume him or gather him in my desperate clutches.
And so I sighed, letting him vanish far away into the allure of the world while sitting by the river each day to bid goodbye.

Lily
