Saudade

Wild storms doth chase me nevermore

I am no longer a slave of grief.
I have sought shelter at a sanctuary of spring,
Where the sky is clear,
The sun shines brightly,
And trees have abandoned the fear of autumn.

In a far-off distance, I see your figure
Near a cave of dark, cavernous walls
That attempt to lure me with memories,
Wherein, I once ran barefoot along the rough pathways,
Only to be lost in your elusive embrace.
I now stand still,
I want you no more.
I long to be near you sometime.

My heart is devoid of pain,
My eyes are devoid of tears,
But why does the gloom of your absence reign?
I am imprisoned with invisible chains
Of a strange longing,
That calls out your name
In a voice that echoes of my own screams,
Which were once called our "Love-Song" of passion.
Its tune lingers in the corridors of my mind sometimes.
I slam the door shut,
Once! Twice! Thrice!
To remind myself
Of the misery and the endless nights you brought me.
I want you no more.
I long to be near you sometime.

I dream of visiting you someday,
To paint your walls with the color of love that once was,
And still remains
Silent and secluded,
Hiding in the shadows,
Eternal and unscathed in the deepest voids of the heart
Where it has found its home.

It dares not see the light,
For fear of questions that'll taint it with guilt.
It dares not speak its name,
For fear of glances that'll choke it with shame.
I want you no more.
I long to be near you sometime.

Lily
